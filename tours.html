Mongolia Overview

Mongolia is a landlocked country located between Russia and China. It is known for its vast steppe, rich cultural heritage, and nomadic lifestyle. With a population of about 3 million people, it is one of the least densely populated countries in the world.

History

Mongolia has a rich history, with its roots tracing back to the time of the Mongol Empire, founded by Genghis Khan in the early 13th century. The empire was known for its military prowess and cultural exchanges across Asia and Europe.

Culture

Mongolian culture is a blend of traditional and modern influences. The country's nomadic lifestyle is reflected in its architecture, cuisine, and customs. The ger, a traditional yurt, is a symbol of Mongolian hospitality.

Nomadic Lifestyle

Nomadic herding is a way of life for many Mongolians. They rely on their livestock for food, clothing, and shelter. The nomads move with the seasons, grazing their animals on the lush pastures of the steppes.
